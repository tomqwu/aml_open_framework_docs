Competitive positioning · May 2026

Where this framework actually wins.

Three events shifted the competitive map since April: SR 26-2 became examination-active on 17 April; the GENIUS Act NPRM (April 8) created a new regulated buyer segment (stablecoin issuers); and AUSTRAC Tranche 2 enforcement begins July 1 — 32 days from now.

Author · deep-research-agent Scope · post-Round 6 (PR #72) · 24 dashboard pages · 991 tests Refresh · 2026-05-25 · Previous: April 2026 edition
What changed since April SR 26-2 is no longer incoming — it is the baseline. The April edition noted it was "12 days old." By May 25 it is 38 days operative and examination-active. The GENIUS Act NPRM adds stablecoin issuers as a new BSA-obligated buyer. AUSTRAC Tranche 2 (July 1) creates the first OSS-gap in the Australian DNFBP market. The OCC/FDIC reputation-risk final rule forces re-documentation of risk-rating rationale around concrete BSA/sanctions drivers — the Compliance Manifest's regulation_ref per rule is the evidence artefact.

Competitive landscape — May 2026

Commercial enterprise platforms

Veteran rules-plus-ML platforms (NICE Actimize SAM, Oracle FCCM, SymphonyAI Sensa-NetReveal, FIS/SAS AML) dominate Tier-1 RFPs by default. SR 26-2 adds a model-validation overhead to every ML scenario — second-line teams are asking vendors for SR-26-2-aligned documentation packages they cannot always produce quickly. The framework slots in as the independent challenger model (the third acceptable SR-26-2 validation method) without displacing the production engine.

AI-native challengers (ComplyAdvantage, Hawk:AI, Featurespace/Visa, Feedzai, Lucinity): SR 26-2 is a structural headwind. ML-first designs carry a model-validation tax on every typology. Buyers now arrive to the framework specifically because SR 26-2 makes the ML-first alternative more expensive to govern.

2. Where the framework actually wins

SR 26-2 · examination-active since 2026-04-17

Independent challenger model — the slot that opened

What it is: Joint guidance explicitly lists independent re-implementation as one of three acceptable challenger-model validation methods for TM, sanctions screening, and CDD scoring.

For buyersThe framework's deterministic re-run + hash-chained audit ledger is the most complete OSS implementation of that method. No commercial platform publishes a same-Manifest + same-data + same-seed = identical-output guarantee. Buyer: second-line MRM/validation function at any Cat-2+ bank.

SourceSR 26-2 letter · OCC Bulletin 2026-13

GENIUS Act NPRM · comments due 2026-06-09

First BSA-program obligation for stablecoin issuers

What it is: FinCEN/OFAC NPRM proposes full BSA-program equivalents and a mandatory OFAC sanctions compliance program for permitted payment stablecoin issuers (PPSIs).

For buyersNo commercial platform has positioned specifically for PPSIs. The framework's VASP tooling (Travel Rule validator, multi-jurisdiction templating, 314(b)/OFAC sharing) is the fastest route to a PPSI-defensible posture in OSS. Buyer: stablecoin issuer / PPSI, or bank sponsoring one.

SourceFederal Register 2026-06963 · Treasury SB0435

AUSTRAC Tranche 2 · enforcement 2026-07-01

Australian DNFBP market — zero OSS competition

What it is: Lawyers, accountants, real-estate agents, and DPMS become enforceable from July 1. No OSS AML framework has shipped an AUSTRAC-specific example spec.

For buyersAUSTRAC's stated first-cycle posture favours enforceable undertakings for firms with documented good-faith programs. The framework's multi-jurisdiction pattern can serve as the template in one PR — the only OSS option for Australian DNFBPs with 32 days until enforcement. Buyer: Australian lawyer, accountant, real-estate agent, DPMS firm.

SourceAUSTRAC Regulatory Expectations 2025–26

OCC/FDIC Reputation-Risk Final Rule · effective April 2026

Risk-rating re-documentation requirement

What it is: The joint final rule prohibiting reputation-based debanking means MLROs must re-document risk-rating rationale around concrete BSA/sanctions risk drivers — not examiner pressure on customer type.

For buyersThe Compliance Manifest's mandatory regulation_ref per rule is the artefact that evidences the re-documentation. Buyer: any bank that has used examiner pressure as cover to exit crypto, cannabis, MSBs, or firearms segments.

SourceOCC NR-IA-2026-26 · FDIC FIL

3. Five highest-leverage next features

Re-ranked with SR 26-2 examination-active (April 17) and the June 9 / July 1 / July 10 deadlines as the decision axes.

#1 AUSTRAC Tranche 2 example spec · examples/austrac_tranche2/
4 daysIMPACT/EFFORT: HIGHEST

July 1 enforcement deadline for Australian lawyers, accountants, real-estate agents, DPMS. No OSS competitor has shipped this. AUSTRAC's first-cycle posture favours documented good-faith programs. One PR, one new buyer segment, zero OSS competition.

Source: AUSTRAC Regulatory Expectations 2025–26
#2 GENIUS Act / PPSI compliance template · examples/genius_act_ppsi/
4 daysIMPACT/EFFORT: HIGHEST

FinCEN/OFAC NPRM proposes BSA-program + mandatory OFAC sanctions compliance for PPSIs. Comment deadline June 9. Publishing a reference template during the comment window positions the framework as the PPSI compliance baseline before any competitor moves.

Source: Federal Register 2026-06963
#3 SR 26-2 model inventory CLI · aml model-inventory
3 daysIMPACT/EFFORT: HIGHEST

SR 26-2 requires every TM scenario, sanctions rule, CDD scoring model, and customer-risk-rating tool to be in a named model inventory. aml model-inventory --output sr26-2-manifest.yaml produces it from the Compliance Manifest in one command. Buyer: any second-line MRM team currently hand-building the inventory in Excel.

Source: SR 26-2
#4 Regulatory-change diff watcher · compliance/regwatch.py
3 daysIMPACT/EFFORT: HIGH

Hash every regulation_ref URL in every shipped spec; alert on drift. FinCEN Effectiveness NPRM will finalise late 2026 / early 2027, meaning every regulation_ref pointing to current BSA program rules will need updating. The watcher catches that automatically before the next examination cycle.

Risk: false positives on cosmetic page edits — mitigation: textual-diff threshold + human approval gate.
#5 AMLA STR / RTS effectiveness telemetry pack · metrics/outcomes.py
6 daysIMPACT/EFFORT: HIGH

Alert→case→STR funnel with per-rule precision/recall, packaged against the AMLA RTS due July 10, 2026. AMLA must submit final RTS to the Commission by July 10 — locking the standard for EU-supervised obliged entities. One feature, two regulators (AMLA + FinCEN), both with hard 2026 deadlines.

Source: AMLA consultation
